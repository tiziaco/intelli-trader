<objective>
Refine BOTH over-close guards in `itrader/portfolio_handler/portfolio.py` (the spot
guard from quick task 260623-gao at ~:339, and its margin CR-02 twin at ~:428) so
they tolerate sub-quantum Decimal rounding noise while still raising loudly on a
GROSS over-sell.

Background: re-running the full W1 perf benchmark after the A+B fixes, the spot
guard fail-fast ABORTED on coverage instrument C (pyramiding). The exact over-sell
excess was 1E-27 BTC — the last digit of 28-digit Decimal precision, produced by
independent per-add bracket-child quantization in a pyramided position after partial
closes. That is NOT a real over-sell; the strict `>` tripped on dust. Any
bracketed/pyramiding strategy hits this general engine edge.

Fix: change both guards from
  `if not is_increase and transaction.quantity > prior_qty:`
to
  `if not is_increase and (transaction.quantity - prior_qty) > self.position_manager.tolerance:`
reusing the EXISTING `PositionManager.tolerance` (Decimal('0.00001'), 1e-5). After a
sub-tolerance excess passes, normal settlement runs and `_should_close_position`
(abs(net) <= tolerance) absorbs the ~1e-27 residual as a clean full close.

Purpose: Unblock pyramiding/bracketed strategies in backtest without weakening the
real phantom-equity protection (gross over-sell still aborts).
Output: Tolerance-aware guards at both sites + extended TDD regression tests.

OUT OF SCOPE: Fix C (sign-aware net_quantity/market_value — owner-gated); changing
strategy C; sizing/admission/matching.
</objective>
