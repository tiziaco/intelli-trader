<objective>
Implement the two heavy lifecycle verbs on the Plan 06 dispatch skeleton: **D-10 `add`** (catalog-gate,
register dark, persist, warm via the existing P7 pipeline) and **D-11 `remove`** (force-flat first,
wait flat, then drop the object and delete the rows).

Purpose: these are the verbs that make the roster genuinely runtime-mutable, and they are the two that
must NOT invent machinery. RESEARCH is blunt about it: "P10's dominant risk is **duplicating existing
machinery**, not missing machinery. Every 'new' capability in the phase already has a complete
implementation one seam away." `add` reuses `build_strategy` (Plan 05) and the P7 warmup pipeline;
`remove` reuses the P7 force-close -> detach-on-flat machinery. Both reach those pipelines through the
queue (`UniversePollEvent`), never by import — `StrategiesHandler` never sees `UniverseHandler`.

`remove` is the one genuinely new mechanism here: the flat is observed on a LATER event cycle, so it
needs a **pending-removal state**, mirroring the pending-bracket and reconnect-resume precedents.

Output: `add` and `remove` verbs that survive a crash at any point without orphaning a position.

Decisions implemented: **D-10** (catalog-gate then add-dark and warm via the P7 gate), **D-11**
(three distinct lifecycle position semantics; `remove` force-flats first), **D-02** (duplicate-name
loud reject), **D-16** (both verbs work on pairs). Consumes **D-01**'s `build_strategy` and **F-1**'s
`required_base_depth`.
</objective>
