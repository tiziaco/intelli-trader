<objective>
Implement D-01's open-position-on-remove policy: the "block new entries for a leaving symbol"
admission gate, the remove-policy consumer (orphan-and-track default vs force-close flag), and the
detach-on-flat FILL hook. Under orphan-and-track a removed symbol that still holds a position keeps
its WS subscription + feed ring ALIVE (so its SLTP/exit can fire), the engine blocks new entries
for it, and it fully detaches only once flat; force-close instead emits a market exit at removal
then detaches.

This extends `UniverseHandler` (from plan 03) with the `on_universe_update` REMOVE branch and an
`on_fill` flat-detect, adds `_enforce_leaving_symbol_admission` as the FIRST admission gate reading
`Universe.leaving_symbols()` (populated by the consumer), and a new `OrderTriggerSource.ADMISSION_LEAVING`.
Because live order settlement can't reach a fill on the EEA demo (Phase-5 posture), the whole
remove policy is verified DETERMINISTICALLY on a multi-symbol paper/replay harness through the reused
`SimulatedExchange` — not on the live venue.

Purpose: deliver UNIV-02's remove-policy half with automated proof.
Output: the admission gate + enum, the remove-policy consumer + flat-detect, a multi-symbol replay
fixture, and two integration tests (orphan-and-track + force-close).
</objective>
