<objective>
Make portfolio pause and retire durable, and make an account refuse to be disabled while a live
portfolio still depends on it.

Purpose: today `_persist_definition` hardcodes `enabled=True`, nothing ever writes `enabled=False`,
and `delete_portfolio` flips to `ARCHIVED` and drops the in-memory entry but **never touches the
definition store** ‚Äî so a portfolio an operator deliberately stopped comes back ACTIVE and trading on
the next restart, and a "deleted" portfolio returns with its persisted id and its child tables
reattached. Both are money-relevant and silent.

D-32's two flags separate the two intents the owner actually has: `enabled` is **paused** (keeps its
account, always re-enablable) and `is_deleted` is **retired** (releases the pair, so a new portfolio
can run on the same real venue account). Plan 02 built the schema; this plan gives it semantics.

Output: `set_enabled` / `set_deleted` on the store, `deactivate_portfolio` / `activate_portfolio` on
the handler, a `delete_portfolio` that persists, `is_deleted`-aware rehydrate and distinct-account
checks, and D-33's account-level refusal.

| retired portfolio ‚Üí successor portfolio on the same account | The successor inherits real venue exposure it did not create. |
