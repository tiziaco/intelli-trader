<objective>
Land the W3 accounts boundary (D-28): per-portfolio account minting, the structural guard that makes an
unscoped account inexpressible, and the two deletions that remove the last places a
"the one venue account" assumption can hide.

**The single most important correction in this plan is about what actually guards the wiring.** Making
`new_account` a typed Protocol method was initially justified as killing the arg-swallowing trap
structurally. **That is false, and it is recorded here so it is not repeated:** a catch-all
positional-and-keyword signature is the universally-compatible signature under strict type checking and
satisfies ANY Protocol method, and the venue plugin Protocol is STRUCTURAL — plugins do not subclass
it. An arm keeping catch-all parameters would type-check clean under every option considered. The
Protocol method governs where code lives. **D-11 is the guard:** with `account_id` a required keyword
argument carrying no default, no plugin arm can produce an unscoped account without naming one, and the
failure becomes a construction-time `TypeError`. Today every parameter after the connector has a
default, which is exactly why returning a shared singleton is expressible.

This plan also carries MPORT-01's deletions — the link function and its more-than-one-portfolio guard,
and the facade's single venue-account field — plus the D-26 naming guard, which is the one thing the
data-arm deferral does NOT defer.

Output: a real per-portfolio minting path, a required-argument guard, two deletions with a verified
dead-code sweep, rewritten tests, and the deferral todos.
</objective>
