<objective>
Land the first half of the W6 reconcile boundary (D-28): make reconciliation per-portfolio.

Three defects live within twenty-four lines of the session-start baseline guard, and all three become
consequential the moment there is more than one portfolio:

- The coordinator takes a SCALAR venue account and connector, so with N portfolios it compares every
  portfolio against ONE account (D-19).
- The guard reads a single globally-configured symbol, so an unexplained residual in any other symbol
  is invisible — **a latent gap that exists today, at one portfolio, single-account or not** (D-20).
- The scan RETURNS on the first mismatch, so with N portfolios it stops early (D-21 / F-2, confirmed).

The fix for the first is structural rather than cosmetic: once each portfolio supplies its own account,
comparing one portfolio against another portfolio's account becomes impossible to express.

Purpose: per-portfolio reconciliation is the requirement. A coordinator holding one scalar account is
the single-portfolio assumption in its last hiding place.

Output: a coordinator with no scalar account or connector, an all-symbols per-portfolio baseline scan
with per-instrument precision computed in the right place, and an evaluate-all scan that collects
before it decides.
</objective>
