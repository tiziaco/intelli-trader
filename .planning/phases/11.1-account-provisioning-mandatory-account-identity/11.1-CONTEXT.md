<domain>
## Phase Boundary

The live composition root stops computing account identity and starts trusting what the database
already enforces (`portfolios.venue_name` / `portfolios.account_id` are `NOT NULL` under a composite
FK onto `venue_accounts`), closing the Phase 11 code-review findings gated on that decision.

The structural half — settled below — goes further than the ROADMAP text anticipated. The discussion
rejected the framing that this is a *relocation* of composition-root helpers into a new module. The
defect is that domain objects are missing behaviour: `Portfolio` mints its own `Account` and
`ExecutionHandler` mints its own `SimulatedExchange`, and composition then reaches in to overwrite or
alias the result. Fixing that at the source **deletes** most of the code a relocation would have moved.

</domain>

<decisions>
## Implementation Decisions

### Structural — account ownership and the single venue path

- **D-01 (Account carries NO reference to Portfolio — the full break):** `SimulatedCashAccount(initial_cash)` and `SimulatedMarginAccount(initial_cash)` drop the `portfolio` constructor parameter; `VenueAccount` already takes none. The two portfolio-dependent reads move into their method signatures — `maintenance_margin(positions, ...)` and `margin_ratio(equity)` — supplied by `PortfolioHandler`, already their only caller (`portfolio_handler.py:569` / `:581`). Evidence gathered during discussion — `SimulatedCashAccount` stores `self.portfolio` and never reads it (that is the byte-exact oracle leaf), only `SimulatedMarginAccount` reads it at `simulated.py:831-833` and `:873`, and `VenueAccount.__init__(connector, quote_currency, *, account_id, market_type)` has no portfolio at all. The mutual reference is an accident, not a structural constraint.
- **D-02 (Portfolio RECEIVES a built Account — no factory in the domain object):** `Portfolio.__init__` takes `account: Account`. The duplicate leaf-selection at `portfolio.py:176-179` is deleted. An earlier proposal to pass an account FACTORY was rejected by the owner and is superseded — a factory parameter leaves `Portfolio` participating in its own wiring, which is the defect, not the fix. D-01 is what makes D-02 possible, so they land together.
- **D-03 (`VenuePlugin.new_account` is the SOLE account factory):** account-kind selection has exactly one owner. `new_account` loses its `portfolio_ref` parameter (D-01 removes the need) and takes `enable_margin` on its config instead, sourced from the `PortfolioConfig` that `add_portfolio` already holds. Today the same margin-vs-cash selection is implemented twice — `portfolio.py:176-179` and `PaperVenuePlugin.new_account`, whose docstring admits it is "the pre-11-07 `account_factory` VERBATIM ... copied, not restructured".
- **D-04 (ONE venue path across backtest and live — the backtest registers `PaperVenuePlugin` too):** owner decision, taken deliberately to keep the backtest closer to the real live system. Backtest and live differ only in which plugins are registered. The GATE-01 objection raised against this during discussion was WRONG and is withdrawn — `venues/__init__.py` is documented as deliberately inert by the P5 acceptance gate ("imports NO concretion ... importing `itrader.venues` pulls nothing heavy"), `venues/registry.py` has zero runtime imports, and `itrader.venues` is absent from the inertness gate's `_FORBIDDEN` list. Phase 5's D-05 survives intact — `'simulated'` is still not a registered venue name; `'paper'` is, and it wraps the fill engine.
- **D-05 (The backtest venue name becomes `'paper'`):** backtest and live-paper are the same behaviour — a simulated fill engine over computed accounts — so they carry one name rather than a synonym. The `('csv', DEFAULT_ACCOUNT_ID)` alias in `ExecutionHandler` is retired. **This is the highest oracle-risk item in the phase** — the venue string on every backtest portfolio and order changes, and roughly six test sites read `exchanges[('simulated', DEFAULT_ACCOUNT_ID)]` directly. It warrants its own byte-exact-gated plan rather than riding inside a larger one.
- **D-06 (`PaperVenuePlugin` builds its OWN `SimulatedExchange`):** owner proposal — symmetric with `OkxVenuePlugin` building its own `OkxExchange` in `build_bundle`. `ExecutionHandler` stops minting one at `execution_handler.py:290` and is NOT handed one either. This deletes the hoist-into-composition step that an earlier draft proposed, and dissolves the compose-versus-venue-assembly cycle at its source — `PaperVenuePlugin(execution_handler.exchanges[...])` at `live_trading_system.py:2028` has nothing left to reach for.
- **D-07 (`rng` joins `EngineContext`):** consequence of D-06. The plugin now builds a stochastic component, and determinism requires ONE shared seeded `random.Random` injected at the wiring seam. `EngineContext` currently carries only `bus` / `config` / `environment` / `feed` / `store` / `sql_engine`, so the field is added rather than the rng being re-derived per plugin.
- **D-08 (A memoized `VenueBundles` provider, held by BOTH handlers):** one small provider over `(registry, connectors, ctx)` memoizing `build_bundle` per `(venue, account_id)`. `ExecutionHandler` and `PortfolioHandler` each receive it and ask for the view they need — the exchange, or the account. Nothing is passed in pre-built and nothing is mutated from outside. The owner's first form — `ExecutionHandler` owning the registry directly — was rejected for a concrete reason: two independent `build_bundle` callers produce two `OkxExchange` instances per account, and `OkxExchange.connect()` is the sole spawn site for `_stream_fills` / `_stream_orders`, so the fill streams would double-spawn. This REPLACES `assemble_venues`' eager map plus the `exchanges[...] = ...` registration loop at `live_trading_system.py:2101` — a swap, not an addition.

### Consequences that follow from D-01..D-08 (for the planner, not re-decisions)

- **ACCT-05 is satisfied structurally, not by a new call site.** There is one portfolio creation path and it cannot produce an account-less portfolio. The requirement's "attach on every creation path" framing is obsolete — there is nothing to attach.
- **Deletions (~360 lines, no new module, no new Protocol):** `_attach_venue_accounts` (116), `_account_ids_for_spec` (87, ACCT-01 replaces it with a store query), `_mint_account_rows` (50, ACCT-02 replaces it with a provisioning scope), `_read_account_secret_ref` (43, moves to the store), `_build_account_specs` (38), `_venue_of` (19), `portfolio.py:176-179`, the `compose.py:238-241` reach-in for the commission estimator, and the `:2101` registration loop.
- **`_venue_of` is already redundant today** — it computes `venue_name or exchange`, but `portfolio.py:112` already does `self.exchange = venue_name if venue_name is not None else exchange`. Its only two callers (`:1510`, `:1688`) are both inside functions being deleted.
- **No `trading_system/venue_wiring.py` module is created.** The extraction that opened this discussion is unnecessary once the code stops existing.
- **ACCT-01 factual correction:** the ROADMAP criterion cites `VenueAccountStore.read_enabled_for(venue_name)`, which **does not exist**. The store has `read_all()` (unscoped) and `get(venue, account)`. Writing that venue-scoped enabled-only reader is part of ACCT-01, and the "CR-01 venue filter becomes redundant" claim only holds once it does.
- **ACCT-04 line-number drift:** the ROADMAP cites `live_trading_system.py:1382,1530,2062,2082`; actual sites are `:1402,1550,2082,2102` (drifted ~20 lines via quick tasks `260722-g6w` / `260722-hpz`).

<specifics>
## Specific Ideas

- The owner's framing, which drove the whole redesign: *"shouldn't they be dissolved in the relative handler classes? I feel that with your proposed solution we just move the mess around, when the underlying problem is that maybe something is missing at the source."* Three proposals were rejected on this basis before the design converged.
- The owner's stance on the backtest: *"I'm ok with including venue in backtest if this helps to stay closer to the real live system"* — parity is preferred over minimising the backtest's dependency surface, given the substrate is provably inert.
- The governing principle, to be preserved in-code where these decisions land: **objects receive their collaborators; composition constructs them.** Nobody mints a collaborator whose type someone else decides. This is the same rule as Phase 12's COMP-06 (every collaborator a constructor argument, zero `Optional[Any] = None` wiring fields), applied one level down — so this phase makes Phase 12 smaller rather than adding to it.

</specifics>

<deferred>
## Deferred Ideas

- **Registering `'simulated'` itself as a venue plugin** — rejected, not deferred. A `VenuePlugin` models an external venue with credentials, a connector, and account truth living elsewhere; the simulated exchange is the engine's own matcher. Forcing it into a live-only abstraction for symmetry would drag that abstraction onto the oracle path for no correctness gain. Phase 5's D-05 stands.
- **`AccountProvider` Protocol in `core/`** — designed during discussion, then made unnecessary by D-04. Revisit only if the backtest ever needs to avoid the venue substrate.
- **COMP-03 (Phase 12)** — `config_router.py:402::_dry_validate_copy` and `live_trading_system.py:1218::_dry_validate_config_ingress` are two implementations of one validation contract. Already tracked as Phase 12's COMP-03; not folded here.
- **Phase 12 relationship** — the `venue_wiring.py` / `account_provisioning.py` extraction that opened this discussion is withdrawn. Phase 12's COMP-01 (dissolving `build_live_system`) is unaffected and gets easier, since ~360 lines of what it would have had to dissolve stop existing here.

</deferred>
