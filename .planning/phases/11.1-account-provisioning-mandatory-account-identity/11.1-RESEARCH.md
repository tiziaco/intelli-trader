### Locked Decisions

Copied verbatim from `11.1-CONTEXT.md`. **These are LOCKED and not re-litigated by this research.**
Where a locked decision rests on a factual claim about the code that turns out to be false, that is
recorded under *Critical Findings* below — the decision stands, the plan must absorb the correction.

- **D-01 (Account carries NO reference to Portfolio — the full break):** `SimulatedCashAccount(initial_cash)` and `SimulatedMarginAccount(initial_cash)` drop the `portfolio` constructor parameter; `VenueAccount` already takes none. The two portfolio-dependent reads move into their method signatures — `maintenance_margin(positions, ...)` and `margin_ratio(equity)` — supplied by `PortfolioHandler`, already their only caller (`portfolio_handler.py:569` / `:581`). Evidence gathered during discussion — `SimulatedCashAccount` stores `self.portfolio` and never reads it (that is the byte-exact oracle leaf), only `SimulatedMarginAccount` reads it at `simulated.py:831-833` and `:873`, and `VenueAccount.__init__(connector, quote_currency, *, account_id, market_type)` has no portfolio at all. The mutual reference is an accident, not a structural constraint. **Verified this session — the `Account` ABC does NOT change:** `maintenance_margin` / `margin_ratio` are not abstract methods and exist only on `SimulatedMarginAccount`; `account/base.py`'s abstract surface is `balance` / `available_balance` / `reserved_balance` / `assert_funds_invariant` / `apply_fill_cash_flow` / `reserve` / `release` plus non-abstract `restore_cash` and `is_venue_truth`, and `account/conformance.py` (`_assert_leaf_conforms` / `_assert_live_wiring`) references neither margin method. So D-01 touches two methods on one class plus two call sites — no ABC change, no conformance-suite change.
- **D-02 (Portfolio RECEIVES a built Account — no factory in the domain object):** `Portfolio.__init__` takes `account: Account`. The duplicate leaf-selection at `portfolio.py:176-179` is deleted. An earlier proposal to pass an account FACTORY was rejected by the owner and is superseded — a factory parameter leaves `Portfolio` participating in its own wiring, which is the defect, not the fix. D-01 is what makes D-02 possible, so they land together.
- **D-03 (`VenuePlugin.new_account` is the SOLE account factory):** account-kind selection has exactly one owner. `new_account` loses its `portfolio_ref` parameter (D-01 removes the need) and takes `enable_margin` on its config instead, sourced from the `PortfolioConfig` that `add_portfolio` already holds. Today the same margin-vs-cash selection is implemented twice — `portfolio.py:176-179` and `PaperVenuePlugin.new_account`, whose docstring admits it is "the pre-11-07 `account_factory` VERBATIM ... copied, not restructured". **Re-verified this session against a challenge that `enable_margin` is an account-side property and therefore mis-sourced: it is NOT.** `enable_margin` is consumed only by `PaperVenuePlugin.new_account:95`; `OkxVenuePlugin.new_account:269-275` never reads it and always builds a `VenueAccount`. Its sole consumer is the simulated arm, which is exactly the arm with no `venue_accounts` row. `PortfolioConfig` is the right owner. D-03 stands unchanged.
- **D-04 (ONE venue path across backtest and live — the backtest registers `PaperVenuePlugin` too):** owner decision, taken deliberately to keep the backtest closer to the real live system. Backtest and live differ only in which plugins are registered. The GATE-01 objection raised against this during discussion was WRONG and is withdrawn — `venues/__init__.py` is documented as deliberately inert by the P5 acceptance gate ("imports NO concretion ... importing `itrader.venues` pulls nothing heavy"), `venues/registry.py` has zero runtime imports, and `itrader.venues` is absent from the inertness gate's `_FORBIDDEN` list. Phase 5's D-05 survives intact — `'simulated'` is still not a registered venue name; `'paper'` is, and it wraps the fill engine. **Connector seam resolved this session:** `ConnectorProvider.__init__(plugins: dict[str, ConnectorPlugin])` takes a plain dict and `PaperVenuePlugin.build_bundle` deliberately ignores its `connectors` argument (paper has no venue session; `connector=None`), so the backtest passes a REAL, EMPTY `ConnectorProvider({})` — no `Optional`/`None` wiring seam, honouring the standing objection to late-init.
- **D-05 (The backtest venue name becomes `'paper'`):** backtest and live-paper are the same behaviour — a simulated fill engine over computed accounts — so they carry one name rather than a synonym. The `('csv', DEFAULT_ACCOUNT_ID)` alias in `ExecutionHandler` is retired. **This is the highest oracle-risk item in the phase** and warrants its own byte-exact-gated plan rather than riding inside a larger one. **Blast radius enumerated this session — the backtest string is `'csv'`, NOT `'simulated'`:** `backtest_trading_system.py:520` creates portfolios with `exchange='csv'`, and `execution_handler.py:296` aliases `('csv', DEFAULT_ACCOUNT_ID)` onto the same object as `('simulated', ...)`. The full set of sites is that one string, the whole `exchanges` dict literal at `execution_handler.py:290-303` (including the dead `('ccxt', DEFAULT_ACCOUNT_ID): None` slot), the two direct reads at `backtest_trading_system.py:395` and `compose.py:239-240`, and roughly six test sites. Every one of them fails loudly rather than silently.
- **D-06 (`PaperVenuePlugin` builds its OWN `SimulatedExchange`):** owner proposal — symmetric with `OkxVenuePlugin` building its own `OkxExchange` in `build_bundle`. `ExecutionHandler` stops minting one at `execution_handler.py:290` and is NOT handed one either. This deletes the hoist-into-composition step that an earlier draft proposed, and dissolves the compose-versus-venue-assembly cycle at its source — `PaperVenuePlugin(execution_handler.exchanges[...])` at `live_trading_system.py:2028` has nothing left to reach for.
- **D-07 (`rng` joins `EngineContext`):** consequence of D-06. The plugin now builds a stochastic component, and determinism requires ONE shared seeded `random.Random` injected at the wiring seam. `EngineContext` currently carries only `bus` / `config` / `environment` / `feed` / `store` / `sql_engine`, so the field is added rather than the rng being re-derived per plugin.
- **D-08 (A memoized `VenueBundles` provider, held by BOTH handlers):** one small provider over `(registry, connectors, ctx)` memoizing `build_bundle` per `(venue, account_id)`. `ExecutionHandler` and `PortfolioHandler` each receive it and ask for the view they need — the exchange, or the account. Nothing is passed in pre-built and nothing is mutated from outside. The owner's first form — `ExecutionHandler` owning the registry directly — was rejected for a concrete reason: two independent `build_bundle` callers produce two `OkxExchange` instances per account, and `OkxExchange.connect()` is the sole spawn site for `_stream_fills` / `_stream_orders`, so the fill streams would double-spawn. This REPLACES `assemble_venues`' eager map plus the `exchanges[...] = ...` registration loop at `live_trading_system.py:2101` — a swap, not an addition.
- **D-14 (ONE data provider, built explicitly for the feed — WR-07's real fix):** `VenueBundles` memoizes bundles only; composition makes exactly ONE `build_provider` call, for the primary account. Non-primary providers are never constructed, so WR-07's unwired credential-bearing providers stop existing rather than being wired up. **The review's second proposed fix is REJECTED:** wiring `set_halt_signal` / `set_stream_state_listener` on every provider contradicts a documented decision — `live_trading_system.py:2347` states *"The DATA provider stays deliberately single (one feed, the primary's provider), so it is wired outside the loop"*, and 11-09 collapsed the old primary/secondary split for the exchange/connector arms precisely so a second account cannot keep trading after a connector-fatal halt. There is one feed; wiring N providers into it is meaningless. Cheap to implement: `VenueLifecycle` takes `provider` as a required positional (`lifecycle.py:52`) but never uses it — it only re-exposes it read-only at `:82-84`.
- **D-17 (`PaperVenuePlugin` RECEIVES its `ExchangeConfig` at construction):** `PaperVenuePlugin(exchange_config)` at registration. A clean swap — the constructor slot currently holding the injected `_simulated_exchange` (which D-06 removes) now holds the config it builds one from. **A module-level `from itrader import config` import was considered and rejected on evidence:** `ExchangeConfig` is not a field on `ITraderConfig` (which carries `rng_seed` / `environment` / `name` / `version` / `debug_mode` / `timezone` / four dirs plus `system` / `universe` / `stream` / `feed_provider` / `safety` / `order` / `logging` and the lazy `sql`), and more decisively it is RUN-DERIVED — `backtest_trading_system.py:463-466` seeds `exchange_config.limits.supported_symbols` with this run's complete ticker set via `_seed_supported_symbols` (`:67-78`, D-13/Trap 1). It does not exist at import time; a module-level `ExchangeConfig.default()` would carry the wrong symbol set and `ExecutionHandler`'s validator would refuse the golden ticker. Ordering is safe: it is seeded at `:463-465` and handed to `compose_engine` at `:499`, so it is complete before plugins are registered. Principled split from D-07 — `rng` rides `EngineContext` because determinism needs ONE shared instance across all plugins; `ExchangeConfig` is per-venue, so it belongs per-plugin.
- **D-18 (The commission estimator is DECOMPOSED, not just re-sourced):** owner decision, overriding the recommendation to defer. `FeeModelCommissionEstimator` is deleted from `compose.py`; the `core/commission_estimator.py` seam narrows to a fee-model provider (`() -> FeeModel`), and the admission convention (`side="buy"`, `order_type="market"`) moves into `AdmissionManager`, which is its rightful owner — it is admission policy, not wiring. The adapter was doing two jobs: late-resolving the current fee model (a wiring concern) and applying an admission convention (an admission concern). **Late binding is load-bearing and must be preserved** — `simulated.py:775` does `self.fee_model = self._init_fee_model()` on config update, so the fee model object is REPLACED, not mutated; holding the object directly would silently compute reservations against a stale rate. **This REOPENS D-15 from a prior phase** (the `CommissionEstimator` Protocol shape) — flagged deliberately, not accidentally. **Side effect:** the `isinstance(self._exchange, SimulatedExchange)` guard at `compose.py:78` disappears, replaced by an explicit "this venue exposes no fee model" contract returning `None` → estimate `0`. **Oracle gating required:** the golden run pins `ZeroFeeModel`, so the estimate is `0` under both shapes and value-identity should hold — but the reservation path is oracle-critical and this must be proven byte-exact, not assumed.
- **D-19 (Backtest portfolios pass `venue_name='paper'` explicitly):** owner decision, overriding the minimal-diff recommendation. `backtest_trading_system.py:520` changes `exchange='csv'` to the `'paper'` venue AND passes `venue_name` explicitly, so backtest and live portfolios are structurally identical at creation — the spirit of D-04's one venue path. `portfolio.py:112` (`self.exchange = venue_name if venue_name is not None else exchange`) means the lookup key lands on `('paper', DEFAULT_ACCOUNT_ID)` either way; the explicit field is about honesty of identity, not routing. Note this touches a second field on the oracle path, so it travels with D-05's byte-exact-gated plan.

## Summary

Every structural decision in `11.1-CONTEXT.md` is implementable, and the great majority of its ~40
`file.py:NNN` claims are accurate — noticeably better than the Phase-10 baseline. The evidence the
locked decisions rest on (`Account` ABC unchanged, only two `self.portfolio` reads, only two margin
callers, `ExchangeConfig` run-derived and absent from the singleton, the fee-model rebuild at
`simulated.py:775`, the six deletion targets' exact line counts) is **all verified true**.

However, **nine findings** materially change the plan. Three are dangerous enough to break the phase
if a plan is written from CONTEXT.md alone:

1. **`ConnectorProvider` is not import-inert** (F-2). D-04's core wiring move would redden
   `test_okx_inertness.py` on the first commit, because `itrader/connectors/__init__.py` eagerly
   re-exports `OkxConnector` → `ccxt`, and both names are in `_FORBIDDEN`. Empirically confirmed.
   The barrel has **zero consumers** in the tree, so the fix is a two-line deletion — but it is a
   prerequisite task nobody has scheduled.
2. **The `'csv' → 'paper'` blast radius is ~5× CONTEXT's estimate** and includes two sites CONTEXT
   never names. `order_validator.py:117` is an allowlist that would **reject every order** (loud but
   total: 0 trades), and `universe_wiring.py:98` is an `isinstance`-guarded `.get()` whose own comment
   warns that a stale key makes the Universe injection **silently vanish and the money arithmetic move
   underneath a green-looking suite**. The real radius is 27 files, and it includes the oracle
   generator `scripts/run_backtest.py`, which CONTEXT does not mention at all.
3. **D-03's premise is false for one of the two plugins** (F-1). `new_account` cannot simply "lose
   its `portfolio_ref` parameter (D-01 removes the need)" — `OkxVenuePlugin` uses `portfolio_ref`
   for D-11 *account-identity* resolution, which D-01 does not touch. The decision still stands; the
   plan must carry an explicit `account_id` replacement path or it will hit this mid-execution.

The oracle path also runs through a code path CONTEXT never cites: `scripts/run_backtest.py` builds
`BacktestTradingSystem(exchange="csv", …)` via the **legacy direct-construction arm**
(`backtest_trading_system.py:119-176`), not `build_backtest_system`. D-05, D-17 and D-19 must all be
applied to both arms.

**Primary recommendation:** structure the phase as five waves — (0) fix `connectors/__init__.py` and
prove the inertness gate stays green with `ConnectorProvider` on the backtest graph; (1) D-01+D-02+D-03
atomically, with the `account_id` resolution question settled first; (2) D-07+D-17+D-06 to move the
exchange build into the plugin; (3) D-08+D-14 to swap in `VenueBundles`; (4) D-05+D-19 alone in a
byte-exact-gated plan; (5) D-18 alone, also byte-exact-gated. Never let waves 4 and 5 share a commit
with anything else.

### F-1 — `new_account` cannot simply drop `portfolio_ref` (D-03 / VENUE-02) — **CRITICAL**

> *"a PORTFOLIO was supplied — the id comes from that portfolio and from nowhere else. Falling back to

already holds `account_id` (`portfolio_handler.py:222`) and would pass it on the config alongside
`enable_margin`. The `_account_id_for` "no portfolio supplied" branch then becomes the only branch, and

### The load-bearing D-06 ↔ D-08 ordering

The orchestrator flagged this correctly, and the code confirms it is the sharpest edge in the phase.
`compose.py` today does, in order:

```
:230  execution_handler = ExecutionHandler(...)          # mints the exchange internally
:239  simulated_exchange = execution_handler.exchanges.get(('simulated', DEFAULT_ACCOUNT_ID))
:241  commission_estimator = FeeModelCommissionEstimator(simulated_exchange)
:291  order_handler = OrderHandler(..., commission_estimator=commission_estimator, ...)
```

D-06 removes the mint at `:290` of `execution_handler.py`. The instant that lands, `compose.py:239`
returns `None` and `FeeModelCommissionEstimator(None).__call__` returns `Decimal("0")` via the
`:78` guard. **That is also the golden value** (the oracle pins `ZeroFeeModel`), so the oracle stays
green while the reservation path is structurally broken. A split landing here is *green and wrong*.

Two mitigations for the planner, both cheap:

- Keep D-06 and D-08 in **one wave** (recommended — merge waves 2 and 3 if plan sizing allows), or
- If they must split, add a temporary fail-loud assertion at `compose.py:239` (`if simulated_exchange
  is None: raise`) so the intermediate state cannot be green. Remove it in the D-08 plan.

The same "0 is both the failure value and the correct value" trap governs D-18 (wave 5) — which is
exactly why CONTEXT says value-identity must be *proven*, not assumed. A meaningful proof is a test
that pins a **non-zero** fee model through the new seam and asserts the reservation matches the old
adapter's output, in addition to the byte-exact oracle.

### Anti-patterns to avoid (project-specific)

- **`.get(key)` + `isinstance` guard on a wiring path.** It converts a key-rename bug into a silent
  behaviour change (F-4). Use a fail-loud lookup on any path the oracle depends on.
- **A `None`/`Optional` wiring parameter to represent "this mode doesn't have one."** D-04 explicitly
  rejects it (pass `ConnectorProvider({})`, not `None`). Same rule for `VenueBundles`.
- **A "0 is both the error value and the correct value" seam.** Both `compose.py:78` and the D-18
  target return `Decimal("0")` on the no-exchange path, which is also the golden fee. Any test for
  these seams must pin a **non-zero** fee model.
- **Normalizing indentation.** See the table below — `portfolio_handler/` and `trading_system/` are
  each internally mixed.
- **Editing only the factory construction arm.** The oracle runs the legacy arm (F-5).

## Don't Hand-Roll

| Problem | Don't Build | Use Instead | Why |
|---------|-------------|-------------|-----|
| Per-`(venue, account_id)` memoization | A fresh dict + key-tuple discipline in `VenueBundles` | Copy `ConnectorProvider.get`'s shape (`provider.py:72-80`) | Same key convention (`spec.account_id or "default"`), already unit-tested, and divergence between the two memos re-opens the double-`OkxExchange` bug D-08 exists to prevent |

| Venue-name registry | An enum or a constants module | `ExecutionVenueRegistry` with explicit `register(name, plugin)` | D-01 (Phase 5) chose explicit registration precisely so "register ≠ import a concretion" stays greppable |
| Fee-model late binding | Caching the fee model object anywhere | Read `exchange.fee_model` at call time | `simulated.py:775` **replaces** the object on `update_config` (G4). A cached reference silently computes reservations at a stale rate |

proposes a new abstraction, that is a signal the defect was moved rather than fixed (the owner's

## Common Pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: The silent Universe drop
**What goes wrong:** `universe_wiring.py:98`'s `.get(('simulated', …))` returns `None` after the key
change, the `isinstance` guard skips, `set_universe` never runs, and `min_order_size` resolution falls
back to the venue default. **Why it happens:** the guard was written for a legitimately-optional
exchange, not for a key rename. **How to avoid:** convert the site to a fail-loud lookup in the same
commit as the key change. **Warning sign:** oracle diff shows changed *quantities* with unchanged
trade dates.

### Pitfall 2: Green-and-wrong on the reservation path
**What goes wrong:** the commission estimator degrades to `Decimal("0")` — which equals the golden
value — so both the oracle and the suite stay green while the reservation seam is broken.
**How to avoid:** any plan touching D-06, D-08 or D-18 must include a test pinning a **non-zero** fee
model through the seam. **Warning sign:** the only evidence offered is "oracle green."

### Pitfall 3: Editing one construction arm
**What goes wrong:** D-05/D-17/D-19 land on `build_backtest_system` only; the oracle (legacy arm, F-5)
still runs the old wiring and passes, proving nothing. **How to avoid:** every plan touching backtest
wiring lists **both** `backtest_trading_system.py:119-176` and `:455-529` in its file set, plus
`scripts/run_backtest.py`.

### Pitfall 4: Indentation corruption
**What goes wrong:** `portfolio.py` is TABS but `portfolio_handler.py` and `account/*.py` are SPACES;
`engine_context.py` and `compose.py` are TABS but `live_trading_system.py` is SPACES. A wave that
touches both sides of either pair will corrupt one. **How to avoid:** measure per file (table below),
never per package. **Note on verification:** a whole-file "no space-indented lines" gate produces
*false* failures on tab files (wrapped docstring prose); scan **added diff lines** only.

### Pitfall 5: The `filterwarnings=["error"]` trap
**What goes wrong:** a `DeprecationWarning` from a reshaped dataclass or Protocol fails the suite at a
site unrelated to the change. **How to avoid:** run the full suite, not just the touched subtree.

### Pitfall 6: Assuming `Account` ABC changes
**What goes wrong:** a plan adds `maintenance_margin` to `account/base.py` "for symmetry," forcing
`SimulatedCashAccount` and `VenueAccount` to implement it. **How to avoid:** A4/A5 are VERIFIED — the
ABC does not change, and `conformance.py` is untouched. This is one of the few places CONTEXT is
unambiguously right and a well-meaning executor could still get it wrong.

### Pitfall 7: The `_account_id_for` conflation
**What goes wrong:** dropping `portfolio_ref` (F-1) collapses `_account_id_for`'s two-rule branch,
silently attaching an unnamed portfolio to whichever account the bundle happens to be for — the exact
D-11 conflation Phase 11 closed. **Warning sign:** `tests/integration/test_distinct_account_invariant.py`
(20 tests) going yellow or being edited to accommodate.

## Security Domain

Trading-engine internal refactor; no new network surface, no new user input, no new credential path.

### Applicable ASVS Categories

| ASVS Category | Applies | Standard Control |
|---------------|---------|-----------------|
| V2 Authentication | no | No auth surface touched. Venue credentials are resolved by `EnvCredentialResolver` and owned solely by the connector; unchanged this phase |
| V3 Session Management | no | No user sessions. Venue *sessions* (`LiveConnector`) are memoized by `ConnectorProvider`; D-08's memo exists specifically to prevent duplicate sessions |
| V4 Access Control | **yes (indirect)** | Two existing access-control invariants are at risk: (a) the D-11 account-identity rule in `_account_id_for` (F-1) — removing `portfolio_ref` would let an unnamed portfolio attach to an arbitrary venue account; (b) `LiveTradingSystem.add_event`'s D-10 default-deny ingress, unchanged but downstream of `AdmissionManager`, which D-18 modifies |

| V6 Cryptography | no | No crypto. `secret_ref` indirection is untouched; no secret value moves |
| V7 Error Handling & Logging | **yes** | F-4's silent `.get`+`isinstance` is a detection failure. New wiring must fail loud (`registry.get`'s bare `KeyError` idiom) |
| V8 Data Protection | no | No new persistence. `venue_accounts.config_json` wiring is 11.2 (D-12) |

### Known Threat Patterns for this stack

| Pattern | STRIDE | Standard Mitigation |
|---------|--------|---------------------|
| Account conflation — a portfolio routed to the wrong venue account (F-1) | Elevation of Privilege | Preserve `_account_id_for`'s no-cross-fallback rule and its raise-on-absent-id; keep `test_distinct_account_invariant.py` green unmodified |
| Silent control bypass — a renamed key makes a guard skip (F-4) | Tampering | Fail-loud lookup instead of `.get` + `isinstance` on any oracle/admission path |
| Allowlist drift — venue rename outruns the validator (F-3) | Spoofing | Update the allowlist in the same commit as the rename; add the missing test |
| Under-reservation on a live venue (`compose.py:78`) | Repudiation / financial | **Known, deferred, documented.** D-18 makes it visible without fixing it — the plan must not silently "fix" it (that would move the oracle) |
| Duplicate credential-bearing providers (WR-07) | Information Disclosure | D-14 — build exactly one; unconstructed providers hold no credentials |
| Duplicate venue sessions double-spawning fill streams | Denial of Service | D-08's memoization — the stated reason the registry-per-handler form was rejected |

## Open Questions

1. **Does `'simulated'` survive as an `exchanges` key?**
   - What we know: D-04 says `'simulated'` is not a *registered venue name* (VERIFIED — only `'okx'`
     and `'paper'` are registered at `live_trading_system.py:2025-2029`). But `('simulated',
     DEFAULT_ACCOUNT_ID)` is a *registry key* read by six production sites and ~56 test files.
   - What's unclear: whether D-08's `VenueBundles` registers the paper bundle under `('paper', …)`
     only, or also keeps `('simulated', …)` during a transition.
   - Recommendation: **decide before Wave 3, not during Wave 4.** Keeping the `'simulated'` key is a
     ~56-file saving but re-creates the alias D-05 exists to retire. Retiring it is cleaner and is
     what D-04/D-05 imply — but then F-4's silent site and F-11's config path must both be handled in
     the same commit.

2. **Where does plugin registration happen for the backtest?**
   - What we know: `compose_engine` is documented as mode-agnostic and must not hardcode a run-mode
     backend (`compose.py:11-16`, D-14a). Registration with a concrete `PaperVenuePlugin` is
     mode-specific.
   - What's unclear: registration in the factory (both arms, F-5) with `VenueBundles` passed into
     `compose_engine`, versus registration inside compose.
   - Recommendation: **factory-side**, matching D-14a and D-17's ordering evidence. `compose_engine`
     gains a `venue_bundles` parameter and likely *loses* `exchange_config` (which becomes the
     plugin's).

3. **What is the D-18 Protocol's concrete shape?** (F-7)
   - Recommendation: `TYPE_CHECKING`-guarded forward-ref to `FeeModel`, matching
     `engine_context.py:45-57`. Settle this in the plan, not in the executor's head.

4. **Does `_build_account_specs` get restructured or deleted in 11.1?** (F-10)
   - What we know: it calls two functions explicitly assigned to 11.2.
   - Recommendation: restructure (keep the account-id enumeration, drop the parts `VenueBundles`
     subsumes) and record the realistic ≈186-line deletion budget rather than CONTEXT's ~360.

5. **Should `VenueBundles` live in `venues/` or `trading_system/`?**
   - What we know: CONTEXT says "no new module" is created but also describes a "small provider" —
     these are in mild tension. `venues/` is import-inert (C1–C3) and is the natural home; the
     deferred "consolidate the venue domain's six homes" item points the same way.
   - Recommendation: `itrader/venues/bundles.py`, SPACES indentation, `TYPE_CHECKING`-only imports,
     **not** exported from the `venues/__init__.py` barrel (preserving C1).
