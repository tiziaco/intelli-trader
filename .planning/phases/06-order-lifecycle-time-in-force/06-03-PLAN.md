<objective>
Wire the existing-but-unwired EXPIRED lifecycle by adding a parallel EXPIRE arm at each of four
seams (D-08/D-09), each a near-verbatim copy of the CANCELLED arm beside it (PATTERNS.md: ONE
retire-a-resting-order pattern, do NOT invent machinery):

1. `LifecycleManager.expire_all_resting()` — the sweep (peer of `cancel_order`): per-portfolio,
   `order_id`-sorted (D-10); local `order.expire_order()` transition + idempotent
   `portfolio_handler.release()` (WR-04) + emit `OrderEvent(EXPIRE)`. Delegated through
   `OrderManager` and enqueued by `OrderHandler`.
2. `SimulatedExchange.on_order` EXPIRE arm — `matching_engine.cancel(order_id)` bool guard then
   `FillEvent.new_fill('EXPIRED', ..., commission=Decimal("0"))`.
3. `ReconcileManager` EXPIRED arm — `_classify` line + `_apply_expired` + dispatch `elif`, slotting
   into the per-status shape with the try/finally/should_release skeleton BYTE-IDENTICAL (Phase 5
   D-06 discipline). LANDMINE: idempotent on the already-locally-EXPIRED mirror with NO explicit
   guard (`add_state_change` returns False on EXPIRED->EXPIRED, confirmed order.py:307-309).
4. `BacktestRunner._run_backtest` — invoke the sweep AFTER the for-loop, then ONE final
   `process_events()` drain (provably non-cascading: routes literal sends ORDER->on_order only and
   FILL->portfolio+reconcile only; EXPIRE emits no SIGNAL/new ORDER).

This phase DOES touch the FRAGILE `reconcile/` path — small, well-scoped, under the owner-gate.

Purpose: Run-end resting orders transition to EXPIRED; nothing lingers PENDING (Success Criterion 1).
Output: Four wired EXPIRE arms + Wave-0 unit/integration coverage for sweep, exchange arm, reconcile
arm + idempotency, and the non-cascade assertion.
</objective>
