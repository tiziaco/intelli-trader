# M5a Golden Re-freeze — Next-Bar-Open Fill Convention (D-01/D-13, D-21/D-23)

**Plan:** 06-06 — the phase's ONE result-changing workstream.
**Status:** APPROVED by owner (D-23 blocking checkpoint passed, 2026-06-06).
The flip, the re-frozen goldens and this note land as ONE commit (D-21).

## WHAT changed

Market orders no longer fill immediately at the decision-bar close. Every NEW
order — market included — now rests in the `MatchingEngine` book
(`SimulatedExchange.on_order` routes ALL validated NEW orders to
`matching_engine.submit`; the `execution_timing == "immediate"` branch and the
`execute_order` immediate-fill path are deleted). A market order decided at
tick T fills at the **open of the bar stamped T+1tf**, and `FillEvent.time`
carries the matching bar's time (T+1tf), never the decision tick.

Consequences visible in the reference output:

- **Every entry and exit timestamp shifts exactly +1 bar** (verified: 134/134
  entry dates and 134/134 exit dates moved exactly +1 day; no other shift).
- **Every fill price moves from the decision bar's Close to the next bar's
  Open** (spot-checked below against the raw CSV).
- **Same-bar bracket rule** (Open Question 1, accepted): a parent market fill
  at bar N+1's open does not shield its SL/TP children — they may trigger
  against the same bar's high/low (real-exchange semantics; STOP-beats-LIMIT
  arbitrates double triggers; parent fill is emitted before child fills).
- **Last-bar edge** (bar-timing contract rule 7): an order decided on the
  final dataset bar never fills — no next bar exists. In this run no trade
  disappeared (trade count unchanged); the final exit, decided on 2026-06-02,
  now fills on 2026-06-03 (the dataset's last bar).

## WHY

The same-bar-close fill was look-ahead: the strategy decides on the close of
bar T and was being filled at that same close — trading on information it
could not have had. Next-bar-open is the industry-standard look-ahead-free
convention and the default of both Phase 8 cross-validation reference engines
(`backtesting.py`, `backtrader`), making the M5a reference like-for-like
comparable.

## Pitfall 6 — reservation vs gap-up settle: FINDING

The admission gate reserves `decision_close × qty + est. commission`; a gap-up
next-open fill settles HIGHER than the reservation. **No rejection occurs**:

- Unit-locked in `tests/unit/portfolio/test_cash_reservations.py` — the
  settlement debit succeeds (the funds invariant checks the ledger *balance*,
  never the reservation-adjusted buying power) and the terminal release frees
  the exact reserved amount, idempotently.
- Empirically exercised in the golden run itself: trade 122 entered +4.25 %
  above its reserved decision-close estimate (111279.22 → 116010.01) and
  settled cleanly. The A4 assumption from RESEARCH holds; no Phase 5 behavior
  change was needed.

`OrderEvent.price` is now documented as a DECISION-PRICE ESTIMATE (pre-trade
gate, not a fill ceiling) in `itrader/events_handler/events/order.py`.
